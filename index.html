Time Dilation

Explanation

This is a small simulation to illustrate the concept of relativistic time dilation. What is relativistic time dilation? Essentially the law states that the closer you get to the speed of light, the slower time moves for you. Lets say that you leave earth going 50% the speed of light. To a stationary observer on earth, your clock would appear to tick slower; 1 second would appear to be 1.15 seconds.

In the equation above: t is the time seen by a stationary observer. t0 is the time in a rest frame. v is your velocity and c is the speed of light. So for example if you want to see what 1 sec feels like close to the speed of light, t0 becomes 1 sec, and v becomes the speed of light (186282 miles/second). Solve for t.
